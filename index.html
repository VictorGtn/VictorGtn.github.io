AlphaSurf
ON-THE-FLY SURFACE COMPUTATIONS FOR PROTEIN REPRESENTATION LEARNING  ·  LMRL @ ICLR 2026  ·  Gertner · Cazals · Mallet
Problem

Surface-based protein ML achieves state-of-the-art results, but MSMS-derived surfaces are expensive to compute and produce oversized meshes — a bottleneck for adoption.

Insight

Selected simplices of Kα encode the combinatorial structure of SAS/SES directly (caps, arcs, intersection points), so we can skip expensive mesh-generation heuristics and build learning-ready surfaces on the fly in O(n log n).

Δ ∈ Kα ⟺ ∩ Rj,α ≠ ∅
Result

AlphaSurf replaces MSMS with α-complex meshes on-the-fly. Matches accuracy. Cuts preprocessing 5× with 14× fewer vertices.

Repro details

Dataset: MaSIF-Ligand (1,438 proteins, 7 classes).

Hardware: standard GPU machine with 8 CPU workers for preprocessing; V100 GPU for model processing.

Training setup: same architecture and learning protocol as AtomSurf baseline.

Reporting: balanced accuracy across 3 seeds; preprocessing and processing times reported separately.
